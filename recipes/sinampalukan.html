Sinampalukang Manok

Sinampalukang Manok is the dish that, when I taste it, brings me back home wherever I am. Each spoonful of its sour broth brings back a stream of good memories. It will always have a special place in my heart. My version is simplified and less delicious compared to what my grandma used to cook, but its effect on me is still the same. It's the perfect food to have during the cold and rainy season. It's the kind of food that gives you warmth and comfort, and never disappoints.

Ingredients

Steps

  1. Saute garlic, onion, and ginger until soft and fragrant.
  2. In the same pan, sear the chicken for 1 minute on each side.
  3. Add the water, sampalok mix, fish sauce, Knorr chicken cube, and mustard greens, then stir. Bring to a boil.
  4. Turn down the heat to a simmer.
  5. Wait until the chicken is tender and the broth reaches your desired consistency. Occasionally flip the chicken to cook the other side evenly.
  6. Turn off the heat.
  7. Serve hot and Enjoy!
