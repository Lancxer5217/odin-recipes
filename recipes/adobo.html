Adobong Manok

Adobong Manok (Chicken Adobo) is one of the most iconic Filipino dishes out there! For me, it's also one of the easiest to cook. It can last for days without refrigeration, making it incredibly practical. With its perfect balance of savory, tangy, and garlicky goodness, it's comfort food at its finest.

Ingredients

Steps

  1. Marinate the chicken with soy sauce and crushed garlic, for minimum of 3 hours but best left overnight.
  2. Pour the cooking oil into a pan and heat it.
  3. Sear the chicken for 2 minutes on each side. Searing adds flavor and helps the meat hold its shape during simmering.
  4. Pour in the remaining marinade with the garlic, and add the water. Then, bring it to a boil.
  5. Turn the heat down to a simmer.
  6. Add the dried bay leaves, fine black pepper, and vinegar, then stir. (You can add the cubed potato at this step; just don't put it in too early, or it will become mushy.)
  7. Wait until the chicken is tender or until the sauce reaches your desired consistency. Occasionally flip the chicken to cook the other side evenly.
  8. Turn off the heat.
  9. Serve hot and enjoy!
